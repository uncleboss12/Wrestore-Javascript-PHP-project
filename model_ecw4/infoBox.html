What do these numbers mean?

The list below provides information on the characteristics of the sub-basin selected and details on the various conservation practices proposed for the sub-basin.

The various attributes of conservation practices are explained in the following manner in the list:

  1. Strip cropping: This is implemented only on the agricultural areas of the sub-basin. “Yes” indicates that strip cropping is proposed and implemented in the selected sub-basin’s fields.
  2. Crop rotation: This is implemented only on the agricultural areas of the sub-basin. “Yes” indicates that crop rotation is proposed and implemented in the selected sub-basin’s fields.
  3. Cover crop: This is implemented only on the agricultural areas of the sub-basin. “Yes” indicates that cover crop is proposed and implemented in the selected sub-basin’s fields.
  4. Filter strip width: This indicates the width of filter strips proposed in this selected sub-basin. Width units are in feet.
  5. Grassed waterways: These are proposed only in first order streams. “Yes” indicates that the grassed waterways are proposed and implemented in the selected sub-basin’s streams.
  6. Conservation tillage: This includes the “no-till” practice implemented only on the agricultural areas of the sub-basin. “Yes” indicates that no-till is proposed and implemented in the selected sub-basin’s fields
  7. Wetlands area: This is the total area of all the new accumulated wetlands proposed and implemented in this sub-basin. Area units are in acres.
  8. Wetlands drainage area fraction: This is the fraction of the sub-basin area that drains into the new accumulated wetlands in this sub-basin.
